Tape Shapes

Tape Shapes are one of the simplest and easiest to replicate and also one of the most useful of the Action Geometry constructions.

If you are starting from scratch and have access to a printer but not an existing set of tapeshapes, you can print out the following svg files and cut them out. If printed one to one in size, they should print to 3 inches on a side, assuming 100 px is 1 inch.

The tools needed for this construction are clear packing tape, printed sheets of paper, crayons, a pen, and scissors, as shown here:

Once you have these shapes printed, colored, laminated, and cut out, they are self-replicating. In order to make another set you no longer need a printer, just a pen and the other non electronic art supplies mentioned here. Also these shapes are all the basis for replications into other media, such as wood, brick, stone, pavement, aluminum and plastic, all of which will be discussed elsewhere in other AG scrolls.
